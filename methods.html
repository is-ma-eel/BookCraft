Methods of Binding

These are skills one can employ in the fastening of sheets into a protective cover. It can also be termed as stitching method. Though stitching deals with sewing but it can also be achieved by gluing or sampling with teh aid of a stapler.

Saddle Stitch Binding

This method involve folding sheets together one inside the other and then stapling through the fold line with wire staples.

In this method, sheets are put together into a protective cover and stitched together through the spine with stapled pin.

Process/Steps
  1. Fold sheets and cover into folios
  2. Collate folios and insert them into the cover.
  3. Mark stitching marks on the spine.
  4. Create holes through the spine with the bodkin to ease the passage of the threaded needle.
  5. Use the threaded needle to sew as desired.
    • Label the perforated points A, B, C and D.
    • Start sewing from the center spread by inserting the threaded needle in B to the cover.
    • Insert through C from the cover to the center spread.
    • Pass through D to the cover.
    • Pass through C again to the center spread.
    • Pass through A to the cover.
    • Come through B from the cover and make knot, making sure that the thread from C to A is under the knot.
  6. Mark and trim the edges appropriately.
  7. Cover the spine with binding cloth to cover the stitching thread/stitching marks/stapling pins

Side Stitch Binding

This method uses wire or staple that is stabbed into the front cover, through the inside pages and back cover of the document, instead of along the spine.

The pages/sheets are stitched together into a protective cover by the side.

NB: You can sew through the verso instead of stapling.
Process/Steps
  1. Collate sheets together into two sheets of cover and hold them together with paper clips.
  2. Mark stitching marks and points on the verso.
  3. Label the marked points A, B, C and D.
  4. Create holes on the stitching points with the awl to ease the passage of the needle.
  5. Sew the sheets together with threaded needle.
    • Start sewing by inserting the threaded needle in C from the front cover to the back.
    • Pass through D to the front cover.
    • Pass through B to the back cover.
    • Pass through A from the back to the front cover.
    • Pass through B again to the back cover.
    • Insert through C again to the front cover.
    • Make a knot at C, making sure that the thread from D to B is under the knot.
  6. Cover the spine with binding cloth to cover the stitched area.
  7. Trim the edge of the sheets.

Multi-Section Stitch Binding

In this method, sections are sewn togethr into a book by joining previous sewn sections at the head and tail using kettle or blanket stitching style.

This stitching method is just like the saddle but after sewing, a section thread is not fastened in the middle but joined to a fresh section. It is binding used for binding note books, case bound books, etc.

Process/Steps
  1. Cut and fold sheets of papers into folios.
  2. Collate folios and insert them into one another to form signatures.
  3. Mark and create stitching holes through the spine with the awl.
  4. Arrange collated sections on a stitching frame.
  5. Place folio on both ends of the arranged sections.
  6. Start sewing with one of the end paper from the head to the tail or vice versa.
  7. The sewing is done from the end paper below to the one at the top of the sections by joining previous sewn sections at the head or tail using kettle stitching style.
    • Label the perforated holes/points A, B, C, D, E and F.
    • Start sewing from the end paper and fly leave. Begin by inserting threaded needle in point A at the head from the outside to the inside.
    • Pass through B from the inside.
    • Pass threaded needle on the tape through point C to the inside of the section.
    • Pass the needle through point D to the outside.
    • Pass the needle on the tape through point E to the inside of the section as you did from B to C.
    • Bring the threaded needle out of the section through F.
  8. Place the next section and continue with the sewing from the tail to the head.
  9. Place the next section and continue with the sewing from the head to the tail.
  10. Having stitched it, cover the spine with binding cloth to cover the stitching marks.
  11. Trim the edge of the sheets.
